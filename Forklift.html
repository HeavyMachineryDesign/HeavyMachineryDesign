Forklift

Forklifts are powerful industrial vehicles used primarily for lifting and moving heavy materials over short distances. They are essential in warehouses, construction sites, and manufacturing facilities.

Key Features

  • Lifting Capacity: Forklifts can typically lift loads ranging from 3,000 to 60,000 pounds, depending on the model.
  • Mast Types: Different mast configurations allow for varying lift heights and load handling.
  • Fuel Types: Forklifts can be powered by electric batteries, propane, diesel, or gasoline, each suited for specific applications.
  • Attachments: Forklifts can be equipped with various attachments, such as forks, clamps, and booms, to handle different types of loads.

Types of Forklifts

1. Counterbalance Forklifts

The most common type, featuring forks at the front and a counterweight at the back. Ideal for indoor and outdoor use on level surfaces.

2. Reach Forklifts

Designed for narrow aisles, with extending forks that allow for greater reach. Commonly used in warehouses for storing pallets at height.

3. Pallet Jacks

Manual or electric devices used for lifting and moving pallets. Ideal for small warehouses or retail environments.
