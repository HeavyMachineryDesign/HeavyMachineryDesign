    • About

    A telehandler, also known as a telescopic handler or telehandler forklift, is a versatile lifting machine equipped with a telescopic boom that can extend and retract to provide extended reach and height. It combines the capabilities of a forklift, crane, and aerial work platform, and is widely used in construction, agriculture, and industrial settings to lift, place, and move materials to hard-to-reach places.

    Key Features

    • Telescopic Boom: The adjustable boom provides reach and height, allowing operators to access difficult-to-reach places.
    • Lifting Capacity: Telehandlers can lift heavy loads, typically between 4,000 lbs to 12,000 lbs (1,800 kg to 5,443 kg), depending on the model.
    • Variable Attachments: Can be fitted with different attachments such as pallet forks, buckets, lifting platforms, etc., for multiple tasks.
    • Maneuverability: Features four-wheel drive and various steering options, allowing easy movement in rough terrains.
    • Height and Reach: Commonly reaches heights up to 40 feet (12 meters), enabling operators to place materials at significant heights.
    • Stability: Optional stabilizer systems provide additional stability when lifting at extended heights.
    • Off-Road Capability: Often designed for rugged terrain, with larger tires to handle uneven surfaces.

    Types

    • Fixed Telehandler: Has a stationary boom that requires the operator to move the machine to reposition the load.
    • Rotating Telehandler: Features a rotating boom that allows 360-degree rotation of the load, increasing flexibility and reach.
    • Heavy-Duty Telehandler: Designed for lifting heavy loads, often used in large construction or industrial applications.
    • Compact Telehandler: A smaller version, ideal for confined spaces and lighter tasks, commonly used in agriculture.
    • Agricultural Telehandler: Specifically designed for agricultural applications, with attachments for handling bales, fertilizers, and other materials.

    Applications

    • Construction: Used for lifting and placing materials like bricks, timber, and steel beams at heights on construction sites.
    • Agriculture: Used for handling feed, fertilizers, hay bales, and other materials on farms and agricultural settings.
    • Warehouse and Distribution: Used to load and unload materials, and place items on high shelves in warehouses.
    • Mining and Industrial Applications: Used for handling and transporting materials in rugged environments, such as mining sites.
    • Landscaping and Forestry: Used in forestry for tree removal, planting, and moving materials such as logs and rocks.

    History
