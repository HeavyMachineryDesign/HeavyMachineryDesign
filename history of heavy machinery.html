Ancient and Medieval Periods

Civilizations like the Egyptians and Romans used simple machines such as pulleys, levers, and cranes to construct massive structures.

  • 3000 BCE: Early tools and cranes
  • 200 BCE - 1200 CE: Water wheels and mill technology

Industrial Revolution

The steam engine revolutionized heavy machinery, leading to the creation of steam shovels, mills, and locomotives.

  • 1760s: James Watt's steam engine
  • 1839: William Otis patents the steam shovel

20th Century: Modern Machinery

The invention of internal combustion engines and hydraulics paved the way for tractors, bulldozers, and excavators.

  • 1900s: Rise of Caterpillar and John Deere
  • Hydraulic systems for better control

21st Century: Automation and Sustainability

Modern machinery incorporates GPS, automation, and eco-friendly technologies.

  • Autonomous and electric machinery
  • AI and drone-assisted construction
